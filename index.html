Production and Properties of Copper and Copper Alloy Powders

Granular copper powder can be produced by a number of methods

the most important being atomization, electrolysis, hydrometallurgy and solid state reduction. Each method yields a powder having certain inherent characteristics.

Typically, copper is melted and the liquid metal flows through an orifice where it is struck by a high velocity stream of gas or liquid, usually water, thus breaking the molten metal into particles which solidify rapidly. Particle size and shape are influenced particularly by the atomizing medium, the pressure and the flow rate. Controlled small additions of deoxidizing elements, such as phosphorus, also influence the particle size and shape.
